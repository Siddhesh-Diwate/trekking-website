Rajmachi Trek

Discover the twin forts and lush landscapes of Rajmachi

About Rajmachi Trek

Rajmachi Fort is a historic site in the Sahyadri mountain range, about 17 km from Lonavala. It is famous for its twin forts, Shrivardhan and Manaranjan, and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding valleys and waterfalls.

The trek is popular among beginners and experienced trekkers alike. It features a scenic trail through dense forests, open plateaus, and past beautiful waterfalls. The base villages are Fanasrai (Lonavala) and Kondivade (Karjat), with both routes offering unique experiences.

Rajmachi is best known for its monsoon beauty, when the landscape turns lush green and waterfalls come alive. The fort was a strategic location for the Marathas, controlling trade routes between Mumbai and Pune. Today, it is a favorite for weekend getaways, camping, and firefly watching.

Trek Details

  • Location: Sahyadri Range, near Lonavala and Karjat
  • Height: 2,710 feet (826 meters) above sea level
  • Trek Distance: 10–13 km (depending on route)
  • Difficulty: Easy to Moderate
  • Duration: 3–6 hours (depending on route and pace)
  • Best Time to Visit: Monsoon and winter for lush greenery and pleasant weather
  • Highlights: Twin forts, waterfalls, scenic views, firefly season, camping
