Lohgad Trek

Experience the historic Iron Fort near Pune

About Lohgad Trek

Lohgad Fort, meaning "Iron Fort" in Marathi, is a historic hill fort located near Lonavala, about 66 km from Pune. Situated at an elevation of 3,389 feet (1,033 meters), it offers panoramic views of the Sahyadri mountains and the beautiful Pawna Lake below.

The trek is considered easy to moderate, making it suitable for beginners and experienced trekkers alike. The base village is Lohgad Wadi, and the trek path is well-defined with around 200-300 steps leading to the top. The total trek distance is about 5 km, and it takes around 1 to 1.5 hours to reach the summit from the base.

Lohgad Fort is famous for its four grand gates, ancient fortifications, and the iconic "Vinchu Kata" (Scorpion's Tail), a fortified spur that extends from the main fort. The fort is a protected monument and a popular destination for day treks from Pune and Mumbai.

Trek Details

  • Location: Malavali, Lonavala (66 km from Pune)
  • Height: 3,389 feet (1,033 meters)
  • Trek Distance: 5 km (approx.)
  • Difficulty: Easy to Moderate
  • Duration: 1 to 1.5 hours (one way)
  • Best Time to Visit: Monsoon and Winter
  • Highlights: Ancient fort, Vinchu Kata, stunning views
