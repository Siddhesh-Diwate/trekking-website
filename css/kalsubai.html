Kalsubai Trek

Conquer the "Everest of Maharashtra"

About Kalsubai Trek

Kalsubai is the highest peak in Maharashtra, standing at 1,646 meters (5,400 feet) above sea level. Known as the "Everest of Maharashtra," it is a popular trekking destination for adventurers from across the country[2][5][6]. The trek begins from Bari village, located in the Ahmednagar district within the Kalsubai Harishchandragad Wildlife Sanctuary.

The trail is moderately challenging and features a mix of lush fields, rocky paths, and a series of iron ladders that help trekkers ascend the steeper sections. The route is well-marked, making it suitable for beginners with reasonable fitness, though it can be demanding for those unaccustomed to physical activity.

At the summit, you'll find a small temple dedicated to Kalsubai, a local deity. Trekkers are rewarded with breathtaking panoramic views of the Sahyadri mountain range and surrounding landscapes, making the effort truly worthwhile.

Trek Details

  • Location: Bari village, Ahmednagar district, Maharashtra
  • Height: 1,646 meters (5,400 feet)
  • Trek Distance: 6.6 km one way (approx.)
  • Difficulty: Moderate
  • Duration: 3–4 hours to ascend, 2–3 hours to descend (total 6–8 hours)
  • Best Time to Visit: Post-monsoon and winter (October to February)
  • Highlights: Highest peak in Maharashtra, iron ladders, scenic views, Kalsubai temple, wildlife sanctuary
